After working for two years in the Arizona community college system, I began my Ph.D. at the University of Pittsburgh. I specialize in the field of Rhetoric and Composition, and my research concentrates particularly on the intersection of rhetoric, software studies, and queer theory. My current project concerns the interplay and slippage between coding languages, interfaces, external systems, and digital texts and how they facilitate and respond to the expression of intersectional queer identities in digital spaces.
