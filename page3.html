The prompt further emphasizes the importance of “wandering” in the process of learning. This is clearly a reference to and a pun on the name of the rhetorical critic Philip Wander,* whose work (fuelled by Materialist vigor) nearly polemically underscores the necessity of critical socio-political engagement in scholarly endeavors and criticizes the academic who refrains from such engagement. Learning is/feels political. Teaching is/feels political. Writing is/feels political.** Grad workers of the world, unite! You have nothing to lose but your likely exploitative and, quite frankly, confusingly articulated contracts!

*And not at all a reference to and pun on the name of pedagogy and composition scholar Neal Learner. That would be ridiculous.

**Coding is also political. But that’s for a later dissertation.
